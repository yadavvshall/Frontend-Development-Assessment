Sugar and Safe Care

Because of respiratory distress, hypotension and an abnormal neurologic exam, Baby Jazmine's feedings were withheld. An IV was placed in her right arm. The hypoglycemia was treated with a 2 mL/kg D10W bolus, given at a rate of 1 mL per minute. A D10W IV infusion was started at 80 mL/kg/day. Her blood sugar was repeated 30 minutes after the D10W bolus.

The repeat blood sugar is 58 mg/dL (3.2 mmol/L).

Temperature

Baby Jazmine was placed on a radiant warmer on servo control. Her axillary temperature was repeated.

The axillary temperature is 36.7°C.

Airway

When Baby Jazmine was admitted to the Special Care Nursery, she was experiencing moderate respiratory distress. She was placed on high-flow nasal cannula, 6 liters per minute (LPM) and 40% oxygen.

Her work of breathing improved and grunting stopped. She remained tachypneic (respiratory rate 72 per minute).

At 6 hours of age, she developed a left-sided pneumothorax. 60 mL of air was removed by needle aspiration. Her respiratory status stabilized.

Her follow-up blood gas reveals a mild respiratory acidosis. She remains on high-flow nasal cannula, 30% oxygen. Her O2 saturation is 94%.

Blood Pressure

Baby Jazmine’s exam was consistent with poor perfusion, and her blood pressure was below normal. She was treated with a 10 mL/kg normal saline bolus. Her blood pressure was repeated.

The capillary refill time is 4 seconds. Diffuse mottling is still apparent, and her neurologic status is unchanged (sleepy, but responds to stimuli). Her feet are cool to touch, and her heart rate is 170 beats per minute. Her blood pressure is now 60/44 (mean 34).

Lab Work

Baby Jazmine's maternal history and clinical exam were concerning for neonatal sepsis. Her CBC was abnormal. A blood culture was obtained, and antibiotics were started.

Emotional Support

Baby Jazmine’s mother expressed feeling overwhelmed by her current situation. Her husband is undergoing chemotherapy treatment for cancer.
