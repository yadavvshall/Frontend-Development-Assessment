Let’s now practice calculating the absolute neutrophil count (ANC).
Use the values on the left to fill in the blanks on the right to calculate the ANC.

Example values

White blood cells (WBC): 15,000 (15x103/uL)
Metamyelocytes: 3 (%)
Bands: 15 (%)
Segmented mature neutrophils: 35 (%)
3 (Metas) + 15 (Bands) + 35 (Segs) = 53% of neutrophils 53% of the white blood cells in this scenario are those primarily responsible for phagocytizing and killing bacteria.
The remaining 47% of the white blood cells are a combination of lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils, and basophils, and they are involved with other hematologic functions.

15,000 x 0.53 = 7,950
The absolute neutrophil count is 7,950

As a point of clarification, sometimes there are only a few or no metamyelocytes on the CBC. The same is true for band neutrophils. That is, there may be only a few or no bands on the CBC report.
