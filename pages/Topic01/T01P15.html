Viral infection may occur anytime during pregnancy or shortly after delivery.
Viral infection in the first trimester

Viral infection during the first trimester may severely impact fetal growth and organ development.

Consequences of early gestation viral infection include symmetric small for gestational age growth and visual, hearing, brain, cardiac and/or liver damage.

Viral infection near time of delivery or shortly after

If the mother or a family member has a viral infection at the time of delivery or shortly after, then the infant may present with a fever, diarrhea, and/or vomiting and, in severe cases, organ failure.
