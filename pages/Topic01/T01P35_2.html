Type 1 diabetes
  • Usually has its onset during childhood.
  • The mother's blood sugar is controlled by insulin therapy.
  • Mothers with this type of diabetes are at increased risk for problems related to placental function; therefore, close monitoring should be done for fetal growth and well-being.
Type 2 diabetes
  • Consequence of insulin resistance.
  • This type of diabetes has historically affected adults.
  • We're seeing Type 2 diabetes increasingly among children and adolescents as a consequence of rising rates of obesity in these age groups
Gestational diabetes mellitus (GDM)
  • A condition in which a mother develops glucose intolerance during pregnancy.
  • Risk factors for developing gestational diabetes include maternal obesity, a past history of gestational diabetes, a maternal family history of Type 2 diabetes, certain ethnic backgrounds, and a diagnosis of polycystic ovarian syndrome.
  • Women with gestational diabetes have a significant risk for developing Type 2 diabetes within 5 to 10 years after their infant's birth. They are also at increased risk for recurrent GDM in their next pregnancies.
  • Strategies to prevent this adverse outcome include encouraging breastfeeding and prompt postpartum weight loss, nutrition counseling, and encouraging moderate exercise.
