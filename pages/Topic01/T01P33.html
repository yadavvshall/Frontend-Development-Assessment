White blood cells (WBCs) are involved in protection against infective organisms and foreign substances.
On the first day after birth, in noninfected infants, the WBC count ranges between 5,300, the 5th percentile and 28,200, the 95th percentile)/mm3.
For all gestational ages, a white blood cell count below 5,000 may correlate with neonatal sepsis, especially if the infant is clinically unwell. The lower the number, the more concerning the finding.
