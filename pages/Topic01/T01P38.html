• An abnormal CBC does not always mean an infant has an infection.
  • An infant with an infection may have a normal CBC in the early phase of illness.
  • The time between onset of infection and the first change in the CBC may be as long as 4 to 6 hours.
  • CBC is a screening tool and should not be used by itself to decide to withhold or start antibiotics.

  • As you evaluate the infant for possible sepsis, always consider:

    • Clinical history
    • Patient condition
    • Clinical signs
    • Other available laboratory data.
