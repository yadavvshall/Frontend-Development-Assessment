After delivery, when the umbilical cord is cut, glucose supply is abruptly stopped, yet the infant's insulin level can remain elevated.

Infant of a Diabetic Mother (IDM) Morbidities

  • Blood sugar can drop rapidly.
  • Nadir is reached within 30 to 60 minutes after birth.
  • May take several days, or longer, for their insulin level to decrease regulating to a normal level
  • Respiratory distress syndrome
  • Polycythemia
  • Hyperviscosity
  • Renal vein thrombosis
  • Hyperbilirubinemia
  • Metabolic derangements (hypocalcemia, hypoglycemia)
IV dextrose may be required to help maintain the infant’s blood sugar at a safe concentration.
